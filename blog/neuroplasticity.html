Neuroplasticity

I will be covering the following points from my research of neuroplasticity:
  • - How understanding the principles benefit people?
  • - How I will engage with it for my benefit?
  • - Ways to increase neuroplasticity?
  • - Link to resource/reference I found useful or engaging

Neuroplasticity creates an adaptive, memory forming mindset that will help you form new neural connections. It has many benefits other than learning, it can help treat mental injury and treat neurological disorders. Being able to understand how it works and training/practicing will help you overcome many challenges throughout your learning experience, no matter what you set your mind to. It helps strengthen and enhance exisitng skills and cognitive abilities.

For me its helped me with procrastination and made me look at the way I learn in a different way. As I learn new things regarding coding, to retain the knowledge and give myself the ability to learn more increasing my neuroplasticity wil lgive me a foot up in this indevour.

A few ways to increase neuroplasticity is to be constantly learning new skills, but challenging yourself as you do this. Managing your stress levels and sleep are also ways that can help (not the easiest and are quite subjective) sleeping gives your brain a safe time rest and reflect on what you have learnt, and staying in a constant state of stress can but your body in a subconscious flight or fight response which will effect your ability to retain knowledge as you are stuck in the “now”.

Growth Mindset

I will be covering the following points from my research of a Growth Mindset:
  • - What is it and why is it relevant?
  • - In my research did anything surprise or change for me?
  • - How will I integrate into my own learning journey?
  • - Link to resource/reference I found useful or engaging

A growth mindset can be summarised in two words, "not yet" not achiving something and giving up is a closed mindset, or just achiving and being happy with the bare minimum is also closed, but the phrase not yet, targets giving something a go again and again untill you get it, or striving for personal excelence. A growth mindset embraces challenge, it looks at putting in the effort to excel in what you do and learning from what went wrong to exceed you last effort. It is relevant because it creates great resilience and creates habits in success.

The concept of a growth mindset is not new to me, as part of my own personally journey I have learned to adapt it into my own lifestyle. Researching it this past week hasn't had any moment that's really stood out for me but learning different reasons on why it is important have helped me grow my own understanding even more.

As I mentioned before, I have already adopted the growth mindset in my study and learning journey, but for me my challenge is always staying on top of everything and not being ashamed to ask for help.
